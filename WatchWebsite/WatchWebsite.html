He saved up his money and purchased a beautifully crafted watch from a well-known brand. It was expensive, yes, but he could tell right away that the quality was exceptional. The watch felt substantial on his wrist and the ticking of the movement was quiet and precise. As time went on, the man noticed something interesting. The high-quality watch retained its value better than his other watches. It didn't scratch easily and required less maintenance than his others. The man simply smiled and told his friend that he had learned that quality was worth its weight in gold. And indeed it was. The man's high-quality watch lasted him a long time and brought him joy every day. It even became a family heirloom that he passed down to his son, who appreciated the craftsmanship and quality just as much as his father did. From that day on, the man chose quality over quantity, especially when it came to buying watches.
